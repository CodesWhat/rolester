## Likely questions + grounded answers

**"Walk me through a complex integration you owned."**
Pulsar Systems — ran 3 concurrent enterprise deployments in one quarter (Salesforce, Greenhouse, Workday integrations), all live on schedule, zero P1s. Cut time-to-value from 14 → 9 weeks by standardizing discovery-to-config handoffs. That's the model I'd apply to OCP's city-system integrations.

**"How do you set technical direction across teams you don't directly manage?"**
At Meridian I set the RAG pipeline architecture and API contracts that four squads built against — defined the chunking strategy, retrieval interface, and citation format up front so teams could move in parallel. Direction through design artifacts + clear interfaces, not mandates.

**"Tell me about operating under governance or compliance constraints."**
The Meridian procurement agentic workflow (Claude API + custom tools) had a hard requirement: no writes without human-in-the-loop confirmation. Built the approval gate into the tool-use contract before the first line of prod code. Governance constraints are engineering constraints — address them in the design, not as an afterthought.

**"What's your approach when a high-stakes system breaks in production?"**
At Pulsar: owned the incident-response runbook for all enterprise deployments. First 15 minutes = triage + customer comms; next 30 = root cause isolation; then a 48-hour blameless post-mortem before the next deploy. Clear escalation paths and pre-written status comms are the difference between a P1 and a crisis.

**"Why OCP at this stage?"**
The platform scope (city-scale, real-time, reliability-critical) is the right level of hard for principal work. The public-sector governance surface is a domain I haven't owned — interesting constraint. Comp aligns. The onsite piece is the open question.
