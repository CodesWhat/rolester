# Forward Deployed Engineer — Aperture Science

**Location:** Remote (US)
**Comp:** $155,000 – $190,000 base + equity + benefits
**Team:** Enrichment Center Launch Engineering

---

## About the Role

Aperture Science is expanding its Enrichment Center AI platform to enterprise customers. Forward Deployed Engineers own the full deployment lifecycle — from initial discovery and integration scoping through production handoff and customer adoption. You'll be the technical owner who makes customers successful.

This is a field role: you'll work directly inside customer environments, connect Aperture's platform to their existing systems (Salesforce, Greenhouse, ServiceNow, ERPs), and stay engaged through hypercare. You'll also feed product and eng with real signal about what breaks in the field.

---

## What You'll Do

- Own 2–4 concurrent enterprise deployments end-to-end, from kickoff through post-launch steady-state
- Drive integration design between the Enrichment Center platform and customer APIs, identity providers, and data pipelines
- Build and maintain deployment tooling, runbooks, and integration templates that make the next deployment faster
- Surface customer workflow gaps and translate them into product requirements; partner closely with product and platform eng
- Operate agentic workflow tooling at the customer layer — configure multi-step agent pipelines, set up tool-use permissions, validate LLM outputs in production
- Serve as technical escalation for launch-blocking integration issues; work across time zones during hypercare windows

---

## What We're Looking For

- 5+ years of software engineering experience, with at least 2 years in a customer-facing or field-deployment capacity
- Hands-on production experience integrating with enterprise SaaS APIs (CRM, ATS, ERP, identity) — you've built the integration, not just specced it
- Practical LLM/agent experience: retrieval pipelines, tool-use, prompt engineering, output validation in production (not research)
- Strong written communication — you translate between customer stakeholders and internal engineering in both directions
- Comfort with ambiguity: customers' environments are messy, requirements shift, and you figure it out
- Python and/or TypeScript proficiency; REST/GraphQL API fluency

### Nice to Have

- Direct exposure to Salesforce, Greenhouse, or Workday integrations
- Experience with agentic workflow frameworks (Claude API, OpenAI function-calling, Langchain, similar)
- Prior FDE, solutions engineering, or implementation engineering title (or clearly equivalent scope)
