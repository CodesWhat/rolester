## About Cyberdyne Systems

Cyberdyne Systems is building autonomous decision-making infrastructure for the next generation of enterprise and industrial AI deployments. Our ML platform team owns the full lifecycle of model delivery: serving, evaluation, monitoring, and controlled rollout — with a hard mandate on safety and auditability for systems that affect real-world operations.

We are not a research lab. We ship production models to production environments. If you care more about deployment reliability than paper acceptance rates, you'll fit here.

---

## The Role

We're looking for a Staff ML Engineer to own two interconnected problem spaces:

1. **Model-Serving Infrastructure** — low-latency, high-reliability serving for a fleet of models used in scheduling, logistics, and resource-allocation workflows. You'll set SLAs, build observability, and be the on-call lead for P1 model incidents.

2. **Evaluation Pipelines** — design and operate offline + online eval loops that give us honest signal on model quality before and after deployment. You'll define the metrics, own the tooling, and work with safety reviewers to gate releases.

You'll operate across engineering, product, and the safety review function. You are the production ML conscience of the division.

---

## What You'll Do

- Design and run model-serving infrastructure: batching, caching, latency budgets, canary deploys, rollback automation
- Build evaluation pipelines: offline evals, shadow scoring, human-in-the-loop review for high-stakes outputs
- Define and own deployment stop criteria in partnership with the Safety team
- Set incident response runbooks for model-quality failures; lead post-mortems
- Review new model candidates against production readiness criteria before any deployment
- Partner with product and research to translate capability milestones into deployment timelines

## Requirements

- 5+ years of software engineering with a strong ML production focus — serving, eval, or MLOps

- Experience designing evaluation pipelines; you know what "good eval coverage" means for a deployed model
- Comfort operating across cross-functional stakeholders: product, data science, safety, and ops
- Strong incident-response instincts — you've been on-call for a model and you know how to triage a quality regression
