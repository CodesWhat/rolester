## About Black Mesa

Black Mesa is a research-driven technology company operating at the frontier of applied science and computational systems. Our Applied Research Systems team builds the internal tooling, data pipelines, and workflow automation that let our research teams move from hypothesis to validated result faster and with higher confidence.

We work on hard problems in ambiguous environments. Our systems are the difference between a research finding and a research finding that anyone can reproduce.

---

## The Role

We're looking for a Research Engineer who thrives in ambiguous, high-stakes environments and can translate messy experimental outputs into reliable, observable production systems. You'll own the full lifecycle of internal research tooling — scoping, prototype, production hardening, and operator handoff — and work closely with research leads who are experts in their domain but not in software engineering.

---

## What You'll Do

- Design and build data ingestion and transformation pipelines that convert noisy, heterogeneous experimental outputs into structured, queryable datasets
- Instrument research workflows so anomalies surface early rather than after a multi-week analysis cycle
- Partner directly with research leads to translate pain points into system requirements, then build to them
- Drive systems from prototype (runs once on a researcher's laptop) to production (runs reliably on the platform, with an on-call owner)
- Own operational discipline: runbooks, monitoring dashboards, incident response, post-mortems
- Identify where AI-assisted tooling (LLM integration, agentic workflows) can accelerate research operations

---

## What We're Looking For

- 4+ years of software engineering with production systems you've owned end-to-end
- Hands-on experience building data pipelines or workflow automation for non-engineering stakeholders
- Comfort working with unstructured, poorly documented, or constantly evolving data sources
- Track record of shipping to researchers, operators, or domain experts who are not software engineers
- Proficiency in Python; familiarity with event-streaming systems (Kafka, Kinesis, or equivalent) and observability tooling (Datadog, Grafana, or similar)
- Strong debugging instincts; able to work forward from limited documentation and high uncertainty

- Experience deploying systems into regulated or safety-conscious operational environments
