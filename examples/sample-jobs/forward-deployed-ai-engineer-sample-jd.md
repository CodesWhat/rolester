## About Thornfield Labs

Thornfield Labs builds AI-powered workflow automation for mid-market operations teams — finance, legal, and supply chain. Our platform connects to the tools teams already use and replaces manual handoffs with agents that can reason, route, and escalate appropriately. We're a Series B company (~180 people) with a product-led growth motion and an enterprise sales overlay. Customers range from 50-person fintechs to Fortune 500 procurement teams.

We move fast, ship frequently, and hold a high bar on reliability — our customers run real business operations on us. The engineering team is ~40 people distributed across the US and Europe.

---

## The Role

We're looking for a **Forward Deployed AI Engineer** to join our Customer Engineering org. This is an individual contributor role at the intersection of sales engineering, solutions architecture, and hands-on software development. You'll work directly with prospective and existing customers to scope, prototype, and deploy AI-powered workflow solutions — then hand off clean, production-ready integrations to the core product team or directly operate them on behalf of the customer.

You'll be the technical owner of a customer's path from "this sounds interesting" to "this is running in production and my team relies on it." That means writing code, not just slides.

---

## What You'll Do

- Partner with account executives and customer success managers on technical discovery — understand customer workflows, integration constraints, and success criteria before a line of code is written.
- Build working prototypes (often in days, not weeks) using Thornfield's platform APIs, LLM integrations, and customer data connectors to demonstrate value in the customer's actual environment.
- Take prototypes to production: harden integrations, handle edge cases, write tests, document handoff instructions, and ensure the deployment is supportable.
- Design and implement RAG pipelines, agent loops, tool-use patterns, and human-in-the-loop escalation flows tailored to customer use cases.
- Serve as a trusted technical advisor to customer engineers and IT stakeholders — explain tradeoffs, push back on bad ideas, and advocate for solutions that will actually work at scale.
- Feed patterns and pain points back to the product team; you're the signal source for what real customers need.
- Occasionally present technical architecture to C-suite and VP-level buyers — you can code and communicate.

## What We're Looking For

**Required:**

## How We Work

- **Remote-first.** Most of the team is distributed. We do two in-person gatherings per year (spring and fall). Customer on-sites happen occasionally — expect 1–3 short trips per quarter.
- **Async-heavy, sync-intentional.** We use Slack and Notion heavily. Meetings have a clear agenda or don't happen. Standups are written.
- **You own the outcome, not the hours.** We care that customers succeed, not that you're online at specific times.
- **Small teams, real scope.** You won't be handed a narrow ticket queue. You'll own a set of customer relationships and have wide latitude to make technical decisions.

## The Interview Process

1. **Recruiter screen** (30 min) — role fit, logistics, comp alignment.
2. **Technical conversation** (60 min) — we'll walk through a past project you're proud of and dig into tradeoffs.
3. **Take-home prototype** (3–4 hours) — build a small integration against a mock API and a provided dataset. We pay for your time.
4. **System design + customer scenario** (60 min) — whiteboard a multi-step agent workflow and role-play a customer discovery conversation.
5. **Final loop** (half day, remote) — meet the team, talk culture and career path.

We move fast. Expect to know where you stand within a week of each step.
